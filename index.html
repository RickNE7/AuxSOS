Subject: Contractual Agreement and Delivery Terms
From: Laura Martínez (Contracts Manager)
To: Mr. González

Dear Mr. González,

I hope this letter finds you well. We want to break the ice with your company. As we discussed last week, our company is eager to get off the ground with the new partnership. We believe this collaboration will allow us to iron out the problems that have delayed our expansion in Latin America.

However, after reviewing the draft contract, we noticed several issues that must be clarified before signing. First, the clause on force major seems incorrectly applied. It states that minor delays in shipping due to traffic would qualify, which is not professionally suitable. Second, the reference to the bill of landing is inaccurate, as the correct term should specify the legal document used in maritime transport.

Additionally, the contract mentions that our team will touch base with your representatives every two weeks. While the intention is clear, the phrasing may not be appropriate in formal business writing. Similarly, the idiom the ball is in your court appears in the section on dispute resolution, which may sound informal and unsuitable for legal documentation.

We also found the use of arbitrage clause instead of "arbitration clause," which changes the meaning entirely. Furthermore, the text refers to a memorandum of understanding (MOU) but translates it incorrectly as "memorando de entendimiento obligatorio", which misrepresents its non-binding nature.

Finally, the contract requires us to carry out with the delivery schedule, but the phrasal verb is misused and should be corrected for clarity.

We kindly request that these points be revised so that both parties can proceed with confidence. Please let us know when you are available to meet and finalize the agreement.

Sincerely,
Laura Martínez

Subject: Contractual Agreement and Delivery Terms
From: Laura Martínez (Contracts Manager)
To: Mr. González

Dear Mr. González,

I hope this letter finds you well. We want to establish a relationship with your company. As we discussed last week, our company is eager to initiate the new partnership. We believe this collaboration will allow us to resolve the problems that have delayed our expansion in Latin America.

However, after reviewing the draft contract, we noticed several issues that must be clarified before signing. First, the clause on force majeure seems incorrectly applied. It states that minor delays in shipping due to traffic would qualify, which is not professionally suitable. Second, the reference to the bill of lading is inaccurate, as the correct term should specify the legal document used in maritime transport.

Additionally, the contract mentions that our team will contact your representatives every two weeks. While the intention is clear, the phrasing may not be appropriate in formal business writing. Similarly, stating that it is your responsibility appears in the section on dispute resolution, which provides clear legal accountability.

We also found the use of an arbitration clause instead of an "arbitrage clause," to properly reflect the meaning. Furthermore, the text refers to a memorandum of understanding (MOU) but translates it incorrectly. It should be translated as "memorando de entendimiento (no vinculante)", to accurately represent its non-binding nature.

Finally, the contract requires us to comply with the delivery schedule, providing better clarity for both parties.

We kindly request that these points be revised so that both parties can proceed with confidence. Please let us know when you are available to meet and finalize the agreement.

Sincerely,
Laura Martínez
